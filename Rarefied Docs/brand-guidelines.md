# Rarefied Brand Guidelines

## 1. Brand Overview

### Brand Essence
Rarefied is an "auditory safari" — a podcast that takes listeners on immersive journeys into the world of rare and endangered species. Each episode introduces a remarkable creature on the brink of extinction and the dedicated conservationists working to save them.

### Mission
To educate and inspire audiences about biodiversity, fostering a deeper connection with the natural world and motivating action for wildlife conservation.

### Target Audience
- Conservation and wildlife enthusiasts
- Podcast listeners interested in science and nature
- Educators and students
- Anyone curious about the natural world

## 3. Color Palette

### Primary Colors

| Color | Hex | RGB | Usage |
|-------|-----|-----|-------|
| **Sage Green** | `#a2d9a2` | 162, 217, 162 | Primary brand color, soft backgrounds, cards |
| **Sky Blue** | `#79b2e3` | 121, 178, 227 | Accent color, buttons, highlights, CTAs |
| **Olive Green** | `#587c49` | 88, 124, 73 | Secondary accent, nature elements, footer |
| **White** | `#ffffff` | 255, 255, 255 | Backgrounds, text on dark surfaces |
| **Black** | `#000000` | 0, 0, 0 | Primary text, high contrast elements |

### Color Usage Guidelines

**Sage Green (#a2d9a2)**
- Use as the primary brand identifier
- Background for hero sections and cards
- Creates a calm, nature-inspired atmosphere

**Sky Blue (#79b2e3)**
- Call-to-action buttons and links
- Highlight important information
- Creates visual interest and draws attention

**Olive Green (#587c49)**
- Secondary text and supporting elements
- Footer and navigation accents
- Conveys earthiness and environmental connection

**Black & White**
- Black for body text and high-contrast needs
- White for clean backgrounds and text on dark surfaces

### Accessibility Notes
- Ensure sufficient contrast ratios (minimum 4.5:1 for body text)
- Sage Green and Sky Blue work best as backgrounds with dark text overlays
- Olive Green provides better contrast for text applications

## 4. Typography

### Primary Fonts

| Font | Style | Role |
|------|-------|------|
| **Gopher** | Serif display | Headings, titles, brand statements |
| **Space Mono** | Monospace | Scientific names, data, accents, UI elements |

### Font Usage

**Gopher**
- All headings (H1–H4)
- Episode titles
- Pull quotes and featured text
- Hero section headlines
- Conveys: character, warmth, approachability

**Space Mono**
- Scientific species names (*Ursus arctos horribilis*)
- Episode numbers and metadata
- Timestamps and technical information
- Navigation elements and labels
- Conveys: precision, scientific credibility, modern edge

### Body Text
For extended reading (episode descriptions, blog posts, about pages), use a clean sans-serif system font stack or Inter for optimal readability.

## 6. Voice & Tone

### Core Tone Attributes

| Attribute | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Informative** | Fact-based, well-researched, credible | "The Vancouver Island marmot is one of the world's rarest mammals, with fewer than 200 individuals in the wild." |
| **Passionate** | Genuine enthusiasm for conservation | "These incredible creatures deserve our attention and our action." |
| **Inspiring** | Motivating audiences to care and engage | "Every species saved is a victory for biodiversity." |
| **Engaging** | Accessible, welcoming, conversational | "Ready to go on an auditory safari? Let's meet some amazing animals." |

### Writing Guidelines

**Do:**
- Lead with curiosity and wonder
- Share fascinating facts that surprise and delight
- Highlight the human stories behind conservation efforts
- Use accessible language (explain scientific terms)
- Include calls to action when appropriate
- Celebrate conservation successes alongside challenges

**Don't:**
- Use doom-and-gloom messaging without hope
- Be preachy or condescending
- Oversimplify complex ecological issues
- Use jargon without explanation
- Sensationalize or exaggerate
- Focus only on problems without solutions

### Tone by Context

**Episode Descriptions**
- Hook with an intriguing question or fact
- Introduce the species and guest expert
- Hint at what listeners will discover
- Conversational but informative

**Social Media**
- More casual and playful
- Heavy use of wildlife imagery
- Quick facts and "did you know" content
- Encourage engagement and sharing

**Newsletter**
- Warm and personal
- Behind-the-scenes insights
- Conservation updates and wins
- Direct address to subscribers

**Formal Communications**
- Professional but still warm
- Emphasize expertise and credibility
- Clear and concise

### Brand Phrases
- "Auditory safari"
- "Rare species on the brink of extinction"
- "The amazing people working to save them"
- "Meet your host" (for Meredith's introduction)

## 7. Logo Usage

### Logo Description
The Rarefied logo features the word "RAREFIED" in a stylized serif font, surrounded by silhouettes of diverse wildlife species including:
- Deer/elk
- Bear
- Birds (various species)
- Whale
- Shark
- Fish
- Butterfly
- Turtle
- Small mammals

The animal silhouettes represent the biodiversity focus of the podcast and create a distinctive, recognizable mark.

### Clear Space
Maintain a minimum clear space around the logo equal to the height of the letter "R" in RAREFIED.

### Minimum Size
- Digital: Minimum width of 120px
- Print: Minimum width of 1.5 inches

### Background Usage
- The logo works best on light backgrounds (white or pale colors)
- Avoid placing the logo on busy photographic backgrounds
- If using on a dark background, ensure the logo version has adequate contrast

### What Not to Do
- Don't stretch or distort the logo
- Don't change the logo colors arbitrarily
- Don't add effects (shadows, glows, gradients)
- Don't crop or remove elements
- Don't place on low-contrast backgrounds
